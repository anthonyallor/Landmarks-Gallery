London Eye

The London Eye was opened in 2000 to celebrate the new millennium. Designed by architects David Marks and Julia Barfield, it was originally intended to be a temporary structure. Due to its popularity, it became a permanent landmark and remains one of the most visited attractions in the UK, offering panoramic views across London.

Big Ben

Big Ben refers to the Great Bell housed within the Elizabeth Tower at the Palace of Westminster. Completed in 1859, the clock tower is renowned for its accuracy and iconic chimes. It has become one of the most recognizable symbols of London and represents British democracy and parliamentary tradition.

Tower of London

The Tower of London was founded in 1066 by William the Conqueror following the Norman invasion. Over the centuries, it has served many roles, including a royal residence, fortress, prison, and execution site. Today, it is home to the Crown Jewels and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Buckingham Palace

Originally built in 1703 as Buckingham House, the building was expanded and officially became the London residence of the British monarch in 1837 during Queen Victoria’s reign. Buckingham Palace is now the administrative headquarters of the monarchy and a major symbol of the British royal family.

Tower Bridge

Tower Bridge was completed in 1894 to improve access across the River Thames. Its unique design allows the central sections to lift so ships can pass underneath. Combining engineering innovation with Victorian Gothic architecture, Tower Bridge remains one of London’s most famous crossings.

St Paul’s Cathedral

St Paul’s Cathedral was designed by Sir Christopher Wren and completed in 1710 after the Great Fire of London destroyed the previous cathedral. Known for its magnificent dome, St Paul’s has hosted significant national events including royal weddings, funerals, and thanksgiving services.

Westminster Abbey

Westminster Abbey has been at the heart of British history for over 900 years. It has hosted every coronation since 1066 and is the burial place of many monarchs, poets, scientists, and national figures. The Abbey is both a place of worship and a symbol of national heritage.

The Shard

The Shard opened in 2012 and was designed by architect Renzo Piano. Standing at over 300 meters tall, it is the tallest building in the United Kingdom. The Shard represents modern London and reflects the city’s role as a global financial and cultural center.
